What is Echo of Mind

The space between
sleeping and knowing

Echo of Mind is a voice-driven dream interpretation system. You speak your dream aloud — in the darkness, in the half-light of waking — and the system listens. Not for meaning, but for form.

What returns is not an interpretation but a pattern. A visual echo of your unconscious mind, rendered in real time through psychoanalytic categories that have shaped our understanding of the dreaming self.

The Three Dimensions

How your dream
is heard

01

Figurative

The forms that populate your dream — figures, faces, bodies, archetypes. Who appears in the theatre of your unconscious, and in what form? These entities carry symbolic weight mapped to psychoanalytic frameworks from Jung and Freud.

02

Environment

The dreamscape itself — spatial, atmospheric, elemental. Whether your unconscious builds labyrinths or open plains, coastlines or voids, the architecture of the dream reveals the architecture of the self.

03

Emotional Impact

The residue — the feeling-tone that persists when images dissolve. Fear, euphoria, grief, longing, dissolution. The emotional frequency of the dream, traced as colour, rhythm and texture in the generated pattern.
